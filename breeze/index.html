Aagaz

This is where acting becomes a reality. A streetplay event where the stage is yours to command, as the lights shine on you! A conglomeration of emotions, energy and passion.
This is your chance to create an impact on the audience that leaves them contemplating for days to come. Cry, Act, Shout and send the message across about what you think are the root causes of all the major problems we face today, to strive for a better tomorrow.
Rules and Prizes

The rules for the competition are as follows: Team size: 15 – 17 members. Prelims: The teams are required to send a short synopsis (100­200 words) of the script of their play and mail the same to dramaticssociety@snu.edu.in with the subject as “Aagaz: Script Name”.
IN CASE THE NUMBER OF TEAMS ARE MORE THAN THAT THE SOCIETY CAN MANAGE, TEAMS WILL BE SELECTED ON THE BASIS OF THEIR SCRIPT AND INFORMED ABOUT THE SAME.
Finals: Selected teams shall directly perform in REVELS once their script has undergone initial screening (based on synopsis).
Time Limit: 15 ­20 minutes. In case of violation of this, the team will be penalized 5% of total score for every minute.
Language of performance: Hindi or English or both.
The team size represents the number of people registered as a team. Only these people shall be allowed to perform the street play. It is mandatory to register your team beforehand. It can be done by sending the list of members to dramaticssociety@snu.edu.in
THEME: You may choose the theme as per your discretion but content directed against a certain political group or religious community is STRICLTY PROHIBITED.
Obscenity (at the discretion of the judges) of any kind is not allowed and will lead to immediate disqualifications.
ONLY LIVE MUSIC IS ALLOWED and the musical instruments are expected to be brought by the participating teams themselves. No electrical appliances shall be allowed during the performance, whether inside or outside the circle of performance.
Teams are expected to perform at an open air venue with audience surrounding the team.
For queries, please write to us at dramaticssociety@snu.edu.in or contact these members.

Time Wreck

The motive of this event is to convince the audience with your cinematic skills. The event will be

based in a fictional situation where there will be X number of people in a situation where only one

can survive. The situations and characters are subject to the theme of time travel.

Rules:

 The motive of the players/contestants will be to stay in their given characters and convince

the audience of why they deserve to live over the other person/people. The one who

convinces the judges progresses to the next round.

 The format we would be following would be like that of knockout rounds. So the total

number of people will be divided into two groups and further subdivided into pairs. The

groups would be given two different scenarios. The pairs belonging to each group will be

sent one by one into the scenario. There would be one winner from each pair who will be

regrouped for round two with another winer of round one. This will go on till there are two

winners from each group remaining.

 The final showdown would be between the last four where the two different scenarios that

were running side by side will be merged into one. The ultimate winner would emerge out of

these four.

Cantilivo

Ever dreamt of building a masterpiece structure? Ever wanted to showcase the real structural

engineering skill in you? Here is your chance to prove your mettle as a successful engineer. Show the

world, what a bunch of Popsicle sticks and glue can do!

Come, be a part of this extraordinary competition where your skills shall let you shine.

GROUP ENTRY: 2 Members per team

TASK

Design an L-shaped cantilever structure using popsicle sticks and adhesive that can sustain maximum

load.

SPECIFICATION

1)The arena consists of a groove with dimension of 150 mm x 200 mm and a depth of 150 mm as shown

in the figure below.

2) It should be noted that the area of cross section of the structure should be same as that of the cross-

section of the groove.

3)The height (H) as shown in the figure should be 800 mm with an error of +/- 10 mm. (Penalty:

Participants will be disqualified if this rule is violated.)

4) Length of the structure (L) should be between 600 mm and 800 mm. (Penalty: Participants will be

disqualified if this rule is violated.)

5) The horizontal part of the structure from point B to C should be perfectly horizontal. (Penalty: 20

points for breaking this rule.)

6) The vertical part of the structure from point A to B must be perfectly vertical. (Penalty: 20 points for

breaking this rule.)

7) There should be a separate slot provided at the end of the horizontal part i.e. between C and D so as

To prevent the slipping of the load in case the horizontal part deflects. (Penalty: 25 points for not

fulfilling this criterion.)

8) The area of cross section for the vertical section i.e the length between points A and B must not vary

and should be fixed as per the above constraints with an area of 200 mm x 150 mm. (Penalty 30 points

for not fulfilling this criteria)

9)The area of cross section for the horizontal section i.e the length between points B and E must not

vary and should be fixed with an area of 150 mm x 150 mm. (Penalty 30 points for not fulfilling this

criteria)

MATERIAL

Popsicle sticks, Fevicol and rubber bands.

JUDGEMENT CRITERIA:

The judging of the structure is based on 3 important criteria: Weight of the Structure (W), The Length of

the horizontal part of the structure (L) and the Load carrying capacity of the structure

1) If the structure carries 'x' load kg before failure, the corresponding team will be awarded 20x points.

2) 100 points will be awarded to the team with the lowest dead weight (W), zero points to the team

with the highest value of (W), and all others will be awarded points based on linear interpolation

between these two extremes.

3)100 points will be awarded to the team with the highest value of (L), zero points to the team with the

lowest value of (L), and all others will be awarded points based on linear interpolation between these

two extremes.

4) If any of the above constraints are not met, point deductions (as mentioned) or disqualification may

be imposed at the sole discretion of the organizers.

5) Score of a team is the sum of the points based on these criteria minus penalty if any.

Tutorial- http://www.wikihow.com/Build-a-Popsicle-Stick-Tower

Casino Royale

Application of Game Theory combined with Skill shall ensure that you take the trophy

home, in this exciting yet simplified version of regular poker.

Rules and Prizes

The rules of the game are as follows:

Two players share a deck composed of three cards: Jack, Queen, and King. The highest

card wins. You each ante one. You each get a card. The third card remains unseen. There

shall be one standard round of betting, with a maximum bet of one chip each, rendering the

following choices: the first player can check or bet one. Player two can call, fold, check or

raise one (as appropriate). If needed, player one can then call or fold (no re­raising).

This classic poker variation was published in the Theory of Games in 1950. And since then,

this game has been solved, which means that there is an optimal strategy which will ensure

victory. Since the players are not aware of this, their best chance at winning is to apply the

concepts of game theory to try and solve the game so they can win.

P.S. Since this is strictly based on skill and not luck, this is legally not gambling.

Rules and Prizes

1.Max number of teams allowed is 20.

2.Two members per team.

3.Max time limit given to a team - 1.5 hours

Rules:

1. Exactly 2 members are allowed for each team.

2. Use of internet at the time of event is prohibited.

3. Designing should be done only by the tools provided at the time of the event.

4. Designing a 2D/3D art is up to each team.

5. Interference in other teams work will lead to disqualification.

5. Decision made by the Judges will be final and binding.

SNU ACM Chapter

Events



Code Debugging

Do you love code debugging or consider yourself to be an expert at fixing errors, nice and clean?

Then surely participate in SNU ACM Chapter’s Code Debugging event where Participants will be

given an hour’s time to debug an unformatted spaghetti code in Python,Java, C.  They have to

correct and reformat the program with zero errors in stipulated time.

Coding Championship

Are you a maestro in problem solving? Or just like to do it for fun, a mere passion maybe?

Do you dream in code and sculpt your sweet dreams in an ocean of pure logic? Then

participate in the Coding Championship!

Wanna know more?

The coding championship is a problem solving race. Participants will be given certain

number of problems to solve using C, C++ or Java in a given time frame. Questions will be

of varying difficulty, and hence will carry different points. The first one to finish all the

questions wins. In case no one is able to finish all questions by then, then the one with the

maximum score wins. Ties shall be handled by a tie breaker round. Questions may pertain to

various fields of programming (algorithms, graphs, mathematical programming questions,

etc).

Hackathon

Flaunt your app development skills!! Get ready for HACKATHON….

Rules:

1. In a team of 2-3, build a web app or phone app during the night based on a theme

( The event is expected to be for 2 hours ). We'll not consider the number of people

in the team while judging, so smaller teams may have a disadvantage.

2. We'll release the theme later.

3. You have to show a working demo after 12 hours at the venue, based on which

you'll be judged. The Judging points would be the idea , relevance to the theme ,

Effectiveness and Ease of use.

You’ll of course learn a lot during this event, as many teams do. 

4. Automated Tools for App Development like App Inventor and App Studio are not

allowed. Using proper frameworks and SDKs is a strict rule.
